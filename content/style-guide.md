# Closure Receipt — Style Guide

> Tone, voice, and copy conventions for the Closure Receipt project.

---

## 🎯 Brand Voice

**Witty, empathetic, non-abusive.**

We're here to help people process and move on — not to enable revenge or harassment.

### Tone Spectrum

| Situation | Tone |
|-----------|------|
| Hero / marketing | Clever, punchy, slightly cheeky |
| Form / input | Warm, supportive, non-judgmental |
| Receipt output | Reflective, validating, closure-focused |
| Error messages | Gentle, helpful, never scolding |

---

## ✍️ Copy Rules

### Do

- Use **British English** spelling (colour, favour, centre, etc.)
- Be concise — every word should earn its place
- Acknowledge emotions without dramatising
- Focus on the user's closure, not the ex's behaviour
- Use inclusive language (they/them when gender unknown)
- Add gentle humour where appropriate
- Validate feelings without encouraging bitterness

### Don't

- **NEVER** suggest sending the receipt to an ex
- **NEVER** use language that could enable harassment
- **NEVER** include slurs, threats, or violent imagery
- Avoid excessive negativity or doom language
- Don't make promises about healing timelines
- Don't use manipulative urgency ("Limited time!")
- Don't be preachy or moralistic

---

## 🚫 Forbidden Phrases

These phrases should **never** appear in the app:

| Phrase | Why |
|--------|-----|
| "Send this to your ex" | Encourages harassment |
| "Make them regret" | Revenge-oriented |
| "Show them what they lost" | Toxic motivation |
| "Get revenge" | Self-explanatory |
| "They deserve to see this" | Encourages unwanted contact |
| "Share this with them" | Harassment enablement |
| "Ruin their day" | Negativity |
| "They'll be sorry" | Revenge fantasy |

## 📝 Micro-copy Examples

### Good ✓

> "No judgement. Only maths — enter whole numbers."

> "Say what you never said. Or don't."

> "Tip: Receipts are public by default. Toggle anonymous mode to hide your name."

> "Closing this account. Thanks for the memories (most of them)."

### Bad ✗

> "Enter how much they wasted."

> "Write your final message to them."

> "Make sure they see this receipt."

> "Time to get your closure and make them pay."

## 🎨 Headline Formatting

The hero headline uses a specific three-line structure:

```
Turn your       ← black, regular weight
breakup into a  ← accent colour, medium weight
RECEIPT.        ← accent colour, heavy weight, caps
```

The word "breakup" has subtle visual tension (micro-rotation or underline).

## 📱 Share Caption

Use this **single approved caption** for all pre-filled social shares:

> **"No refunds. Just receipts — close the tab and glow up."**

This caption:
- Is self-focused (about the user, not the ex)
- Implies moving forward
- Has a catchy rhythm
- Works across all platforms

## 🏷️ Lessons Learned Tags

Auto-generated tags should be:
- Self-reflective, not accusatory
- Forward-looking
- Empowering

### Good examples:
- "Raised your standards"
- "Self-worth restored"
- "Ready to move on"
- "Learnt silence is an answer"

### Avoid:
- "Dodged a bullet" (implies judgement)
- "Their loss" (focuses on ex)
- "Never trust again" (too negative)

## 📝 Error Messages

Errors should be:
- Clear about what went wrong
- Actionable (tell user what to do)
- Never blaming

### Examples:

```
✓ "Please enter their name or nickname."
✗ "You forgot to enter a name."

✓ "Time invested must be at least 1 month."
✗ "Invalid time value."

✓ "Your message contains words that could be hurtful. Consider softer wording."
✗ "Inappropriate content detected."
```
